A customer
4.0 out of 5 starsA day without Brain
December 27, 2001
Format: Audio CD|Verified Purchase
Without a doubt, when it comes to creating musical valium, nobody does it better than Enya.
What you'll hear on this CD is one beautiful, mindlessly flowing melody after another. Fifteen minutes of this and all your worries, your responsibilities, all your guilt & pains, will be completely vaporized. You'll feel like a back country good-old-boy gently tubing down a cool river after 2 or 3 too many Budweiser's.
The track, "Hope Has A Place" - what a gorgeous piece of music - this stuff would cause a Tyrannosaurus Rex to curl up in its cage like a new born puppy - is probably the best track. "Athair Ar Neamh" is a lovely piece of church music though for all I know she is worshiping a rock. "China Roses" equally beautiful, spiritual in message but more romantic musically. A "Tea-House Moon" borders on the moronic, though it is not really all that out of place considering the overall mood of the CD. The last track, "On My Way Home" is very pretty and somewhat peppy - apparently designed to partially lift a person out of their trance so that they can push the little buttons on the remote and start the CD all over again.
Lyrically this CD is somewhat weak (hence my "4" rating - the track "Anywhere Is" is a classic example of New Age psychobabble - which perhaps explains its popularity) but musically it is as rich, colorful and flowing as the gown she is wearing on the cover photo. And Enya's voice - who could do it better?
So shift your brain into neutral, strap on the headphones, and forget about any part of the universe you don't want to worry about.
